History of Blogs

Origins

An early example of a "diary" style blog consisting of text and images transmitted wirelessly in real-time from a wearable   computer with head-up display   appeared on February 22, 1995

The term "weblog" was coined by Jorn Barger on December 17, 1997. The short form, "blog", is attributed to Peter Merholz, who jokingly broke the word "weblog" into the phrase "we blog" in his blog's sidebar around April or May 1999

Shortly thereafter, Evan Williams at Pyra Labs used "blog" as both a noun and verb ("to blog") and devised the term "blogger" in connection with their product, Pyra Labs Blogger. This significantly contributed to the popularization of the terms "blog" and "blogger"


Main articles: History of blogging, Online diary


Before blogging became popular, digital communities existed in many forms, including Usenet, commercial online services, e-mail lists, and Bulletin Board Systems (BBS)
